Your blood sugar is very high.

Take fast-acting insulin: talk to a doctor for dosage levels. Monitor your blood sugar 30 minutes after to ensure it is not too low.

Exercise: Physical activity will cause your body to use more glucose for energy.

  • *IMPORTANT* Before you exercise, check your urine for ketones. If ketones are present, do not engage in any type of physical activity.

Call your doctor if your blood sugar reading is above 300 mg/dL.

If you have symptoms of severe hyperglycemia or Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA), go to the emergency room:

  • Excess ketones in urine
  • Confusion
  • Nausea
  • Shortness of breath
  • Stomach pain
  • Vomiting
  • Fruit scented breath
